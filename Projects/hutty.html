About the Game

A fast-paced arcade survival game with a dash of cuteness about Hutty — a single father meerkat trying to feed his cubs. Players balance risk and survival by collecting falling eggs while avoiding aggressive predators in increasingly chaotic short-session gameplay.

Key Features

Dual-Axis Gameplay

Simultaneous management of vertical rewards and horizontal threats creates constant decision pressure.

Narrative Motivation

The player's performance directly affects the visual state of Hutty's cubs, reinforcing emotional attachment.

Meta Progression

Unlockable levels, collectible cards, achievements and leaderboard systems support long-term retention.

Short Session Design

30–90 second runs with near-instant restart encourage 'one more try' player behavior.

Difficulty & Balance Design

01

Enemy Speed Scaling

Snake speed increases continuously during the session while also scaling between levels.

02

Spawn Density

Threat intervals shrink over time, increasing screen chaos and cognitive pressure.

03

Reward Pressure

Eggs fall faster as survival time increases, reducing reaction windows.

Peak Intensity Window

from 60 to 30 seconds

The game reaches maximum emotional pressure through simultaneous speed growth, object density and reduced safe space. The difficulty curve is designed so that the early levels give the player a sense of control, and then gradually shift the game into a state of heightened pressure.

Design Vision

Hutty was designed around the idea of emotional pressure through simple mechanics. The core gameplay combines fast decision-making with narrative motivation, where every collected egg directly affects the survival of Hutty's cubs.
